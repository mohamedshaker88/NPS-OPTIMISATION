What is NPS (Net Promoter Score)?

NPS is a customer loyalty metric that measures how likely customers are to recommend your service to others.

NPS = % Promoters - % Detractors

It's measured on a scale of -100 to +100

‚Ä¢ A score above 0 is considered good

‚Ä¢ A score above 50 is excellent

‚Ä¢ A score above 70 is world-class

How NPS is Calculated

DETRACTORS

Score: 0-6

Unhappy customers who might damage your brand

PASSIVES

Score: 7-8

Satisfied but unenthusiastic customers

PROMOTERS

Score: 9-10

Loyal customers who will promote your brand

Only Promoters (9-10) and Detractors (0-6) count in NPS calculation

Passives (7-8) are neutral and don't affect the score

üéØ Why This Script is Effective

1. Addresses the Confusion

Customers think "recommend Optimum" is about the company, but it actually affects the individual technician's performance review.

2. Creates Personal Connection

When customers understand their score impacts a real person (their technician), they're more likely to give a fair score.

3. Sets Clear Expectations

Explains that only 9 or 10 helps - anything below 9 actually hurts the technician.

4. Quick and Respectful

Customers don't want long explanations. This gets the point across in under 30 seconds.

5. Timing

Used only when customers express satisfaction, so it feels natural and appropriate.
